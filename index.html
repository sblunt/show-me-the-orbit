The Orbital Elements in Words

  • ω is the argument of periastron. This parameter rotates the vector connecting the star and the planet's periastron within the orbital plane.
  • Ω is the position angle of nodes. This parameter rotates the line of nodes within the plane of the sky.
  • i is the orbit's inclination. This parameter rotates the orbital plane about its line of nodes (dashed black line in top panel).
  • e is the orbit's eccentricity. This parameter controls the orbit's shape and the planet's speed over time.

Symbol and Color Definitions

  • The pink circle tracks the planet's position, and the pink star shows the primary's location. The top panel is shown in the reference frame where the star is stationary. In other words, the orbital track plotted shows the relative RA and Dec of the planet and star over time.
  • The darker portion of the orbital arc is in front of the plane of the sky (closer to Earth), and the lighter portion is behind the plane of the sky (farther from Earth). When the planet symbol becomes transparent, the planet is behind the plane of the sky.
  • The black dashed line in the top panel is the line of nodes, or the line in the plane of the sky that intersects the orbit.

A Caution to RV Astronomers

  • orbitize! defines ω to be the argument of periastron of the planet's orbit, whereas most RV codes (e.g. radvel) define ω to be the argument of periastron of the star's orbit. In practice, this means that you need to add 180° to ω when comparing the outputs of RV codes and orbitize!.
